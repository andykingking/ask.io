How are feature favors selected? What principles inform the selection process?

We are still figuring out the best process for this. Currently we have a small panel of judges who review each submitted favor. We look for favors that we think would be the most meaningful, and which would be best suited for people helping out from all over the world. Usually specific and personal favor requests seems to be best for this.

How much vetting goes into checking that the favor request is authentic?

We haven't really found the need to do much vetting. Whether or not you want to help out and do a favor for someone else is up to you, but we've found that caring enough to do a favor for another is incredibly gratifying in itself, regardless of the results of your actions.

We try to follow up with each favor in the blog

How secure is the data you are collecting?

The only data we collect are favors that are submitted, and they are stored securely in firebase. If you subscribe for updates, we collect your email address, which is stored in mailchimp. We will never sell your email address.

How can I help?

Doing the favor on the front page every week, and spreading the word about ask.io is a big help. Also, if you are interested in helping out with the code, you can fork it on github. Thank you!
